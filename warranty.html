Floating Sauna Warranty

A starting position for the terms we offer on floating and pontoon-mounted builds, with the reasoning behind each choice. Built to edit together, not to publish.

Draft — not customer-facing

Nothing here is agreed or offered yet. Every figure is a starting point drawn from what other UK and industry builders publish. Read the reasoning, change what doesn't fit, and once the terms are locked this becomes the standard floating-build warranty. Do not put any of these numbers in front of a customer until signed off.

The Draft Terms

What we'd cover, and for how long

A tiered warranty — long on the things we build, short on the things we fit

The structure we make ourselves carries the longest cover. Bought-in parts carry the supplier's own term. Anything on or in the water is tied to the marine engineer's sign-off. Commercial units are shorter than domestic, because they take far heavier use.

Element Term What's covered Not covered
Cabin structure & timber 2 years Manufacturing defects in the frame, cladding and joinery we build in the workshop. Movement, checking or colour change natural to outdoor timber.
Pontoon / float & frame Tied to sign-off Structural integrity of the platform, to the standard the independent marine engineer certifies at handover. Anything outside the engineer's stated conditions of use.
Heater, control panel & electrics Supplier's term (Harvia 2 yr) Passed through from the manufacturer. We register it in your name. We don't extend beyond what the maker gives.
Fixtures, lighting, benching 1 year Workmanship on fitted internal components. Wear, and consumables like stones.
Commercial use loading Applies to all rows Terms above apply as written for a commercial site. Domestic-length cover is not offered on paying-guest units.

What Sits Outside Any Cover

These apply across every row above, and are standard across the sector.

✓   Damage from misuse, accident, or use beyond the stated capacity.

✓   Failure to follow the maintenance schedule we hand over on the day.

✓   Owner modifications or third-party work after handover.

✓   Natural movement, weathering and colour change in outdoor timber.

✓   Consumables — sauna stones, bulbs, seals.

✓   Anything the marine engineer's conditions of use specifically exclude.

How We'd Handle a Callout

This mirrors how the barrel-sauna warranty already works in practice, written down so it's consistent.

Genuine structural or build fault

We come out, or tie it into a nearby install, and put it right. This is what the cover is for.

Heater or electrical fault

Handled through the Harvia warranty we registered in your name; we help you run the claim.

Pontoon or buoyancy concern

Referred to the marine engineer who signed the unit off — the same professional who set the conditions inspects against them. This keeps liability where the certification sits.

Why this matters

Routing buoyancy questions back to the certifying engineer means we're never the ones judging whether a float is safe — the qualified party who signed it off is. That protects BSUK on the exact question a careful commercial buyer will ask.

Why We're Setting This Out

01

A floating build carries risk a garden sauna never did

A barrel sauna in someone's garden can fail quietly and cheaply. A 25-person commercial unit on water, with paying guests, cannot. The question a serious buyer asks first is "what happens if something goes wrong" — and on water that question has real weight. Having a written answer ready is the difference between sounding like a workshop and sounding like a business that has done this before.

Example

Sanjay asked on her call, directly, what the warranty is and whether we'd come out for repairs. Right now the honest answer is "we don't know yet for a pontoon." This doc turns that into a confident, specific answer.

02

The heater is one part of an entire structure

The way the barrel warranty already works is that Harvia gives two years and we pass it on — we don't offer longer than the part supplier gives. That logic is sound, but on a floating build the heater is a small fraction of what we've made. Passing through the heater term says nothing about the cabin, the float, or the frame — which is exactly what the customer is worried about. A tiered warranty lets us keep the sensible pass-through on the heater while saying something real about the parts we actually build.

Industry example

This is standard practice, not us inventing something. Flagship (a pontoon builder) gives a 5-year structural warranty but only a 1-year warranty on all electronics and electrical components. Vanderbilt covers the whole structure for life but states the engine warranty is the engine manufacturer's, not theirs. Long on the hull, short on bought-in parts — the exact shape we're proposing.

03

Commercial use has to be shorter than domestic

A domestic sauna gets used a few times a week. A commercial floating unit with 25 people and doors opening constantly gets hammered. Offering the same warranty on both would mean either under-charging domestic or over-exposing ourselves on commercial. The sector already draws this line, and so should we — especially on the very first one, where we're still learning the build.

Industry example

Flagship explicitly caps it: their rental pontoons are limited to a 1-year warranty, against 5 years for a privately-owned one. The heavier the use, the shorter the cover. Sanjay's is a paying-guest site, so it belongs on the shorter side.

04

It keeps liability where the certification sits

We're bringing in an independent marine engineer to sign off buoyancy and stability anyway. Tying the pontoon warranty to their sign-off, and routing any float question back to them, means we never put ourselves in the position of judging whether the platform is safe. The qualified professional who certified it is the one who inspects it. That's the cleanest possible liability position on the part of the build we have least history with.

Ties to

Same principle as the trailers — we don't self-certify roadworthiness, the DVSA does, and that's what protects us. Here the marine engineer plays the DVSA role.

05

A clear warranty is a selling tool, not just a cost

Our nearest UK competitor leans on being "fully insured, professionally engineered." A written, tiered warranty lets us stand next to that instead of looking like a first-timer. It reassures the careful buyer, it justifies the price, and once it's locked it carries forward to every floating build after this one — the same way the deposit gate became reusable. We do the thinking once, then it works for us on every enquiry.

Reusable

Like the £500 deposit reasoning — write it properly once for Sanjay, and it becomes the standard floating-warranty asset for the next holiday park, marina, or lakeside client.
